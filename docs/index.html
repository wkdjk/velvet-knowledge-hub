ⓘ Why do the three sources show different volumes? ▶

Why the three sources show different volumes

NZ exports (Stats NZ) records shipments at the point of export from New Zealand.
Korea quarantine (QIA) records physical arrivals at Korean ports, inspected upon landing.
Korea customs (KSTAT) records formal customs declarations filed upon import clearance.

Four factors cause the figures to differ:

  1. Timing. Sea freight from New Zealand to Korea takes approximately two to three weeks. An NZ shipment recorded in October may appear in the QIA and KSTAT data in November.
  2. Transit and re-export. New Zealand also exports velvet to China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan. Some of that product is subsequently re-exported to Korea and appears in QIA and KSTAT data without a matching NZ-to-Korea export record.
  3. Classification codes. New Zealand and Korea both revised the official product codes used to classify deer velvet in 2022. Data recorded before and after that change may not align perfectly across the boundary.
  4. Fresh/frozen versus dried weight. Korea quarantine (QIA) records fresh/frozen and dried velvet volumes separately. Korea customs (KSTAT) records a combined kg total. A standard conversion factor (0.33 kg dried = 1 kg fresh/frozen) is applied to make volumes comparable, but rounding and mixed shipments introduce small residual differences.

These differences are not data errors. They reflect the different measurement points and classification systems of three independent agencies.
