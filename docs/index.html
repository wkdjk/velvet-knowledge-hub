ⓘ Why do the two sources show different volumes? ▶

Why the two sources show different volumes

NZ exports (Stats NZ) records shipments at the point of export from New Zealand. Korea quarantine (QIA) records physical arrivals at Korean ports, inspected upon landing.

Three factors cause the figures to differ:

  1. Timing. Sea freight from New Zealand to Korea takes approximately two to three weeks. An NZ shipment recorded in October may appear in QIA data in November.
  2. Transit and re-export. New Zealand also exports velvet to China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan. Some of that product is subsequently re-exported to Korea and appears in QIA data without a matching NZ-to-Korea export record.
  3. Classification codes. NZ Customs and Korea Customs use structurally different sub-classification schemes under HS heading 0507. The two countries' sub-codes do not map directly to one another, so NZ and Korean figures cannot be reliably reconciled at the sub-code level.

These differences are not data errors. They reflect the different measurement points and classification systems of two independent agencies.

ⓘ Why do different sources show different import volumes? ▶

Why the chart and table show different figures

The numbers are not directly comparable, and neither is wrong — they come from two distinct MFDS databases covering different import channels.

The chart uses the MFDS annual statistics yearbook (식품의약품통계연보, Table 6-3-8), which records deer velvet imported as a herbal medicine or pharmaceutical ingredient. This is a separate regulatory channel governed by Korea's pharmaceutical authority. Figures show total annual value in USD, 2004–2024.

The records table uses the MFDS food-import portal (수입식품 정보마루), which records deer velvet imported as a food ingredient. These are individual shipment notifications filed under Korea's food safety system, 2016–2026.

A third channel also exists — sliced velvet under a separate product code (HS 0507.90.1190) — which neither source covers. Total Korean deer velvet imports are therefore higher than either source shows alone.
